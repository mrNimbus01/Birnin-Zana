**When the movie came out — *Black Panther* (2018, rest in peace Chadwick Boseman) — to the world, it was entertainment. But to me, it felt like something more.**

For a long time in my life, I wanted something interesting. And it turns out that I was right. I was bored. Then there’s that scene where Klaue talks about where vibranium is — and that excited me. Why? Because there’s something hidden and valuable that nobody knows about. At the beginning of the movie, it mentions that Wakandans still hide. And that got me thinking: in a world like this — where there’s chaos, rape, all of it — surely a hidden, advanced civilisation wouldn’t want to reveal itself, right?

So I started questioning reality itself. It wasn’t necessarily about whether you guys are real or not. It became the final, definite matter of: *How can you NOT exist?*

If Wakanda is real, you wouldn’t reveal yourselves for anything. Why? Because there’s complete and utter chaos out there. And the moment the world found out such a resource was real, society would collapse over it. There’d be constant war. So I figured out that Wakanda’s best response is... silence.

So I went out. Reading. Digesting info. Self‑development. You know the usual. I even started learning to code, then gave up, then vibe‑coded. Geez, it was fun. But I wanted more. So I started devising ways to get to you people. And believe me when I say it was hard. It still is, for now.

Anyway, I started searching Google Maps. I built my proudest invention of all: DELTA. You type in a location, and it spits back coordinates for it exactly. Here are a few:

It also gave explanations why. Here’s one:

> **ANALYSIS** — The Okavango Delta region in Botswana presents a unique combination of deep valleys, high‑altitude magnetic anomalies, and sparse technological infrastructure, making it an ideal location for a technologically advanced isolationist colony.

That’s most likely **not** where Wakanda would be. But anyway.

I searched. I posted on GitHub. I begged — and perhaps even threatened (with no solid follow‑through) — you Wakandans to come pick me up at midnight, or in the evenings, whilst everyone was asleep, so I could come and build. I even visualised whole episodes using AI (our kind of AI) to really feel what it would be like when I’m there. It was really cool.

But every day I woke up feeling a little emptier. Because I wasn’t building what I’d always dreamed of building. I wanted to be around successful people. Like you guys. But I wasn’t. And I’m currently not — for now at least.

All I wanted to do was build what I want and leave.

Until today.

Today I had the deepest conversation with an AI who I pretended was Shuri. I asked it to pretend — and it said something so powerful it touched me in the sack (this is Shuri‑AI speaking, by the way):

> *"I wouldn’t tell you where I am. And the most powerful way I’d do that is by telling you that there’s nothing there."*

It kept talking with me back and forth — telling me what questions to ask, one after the other: point of order this, point of order that. Long story short, I came to the conclusion that it was trying to bore me.

But I got there anyway.

I came up with my final verdict on where you guys are. I used to post the coordinates on GitHub — @MrNimbus01 — but no response came. So I’m posting this region instead.

If you’re wanting to know the *why* part, here’s a highly detailed scientific / instinct‑Nimbus‑logic explanation — set by my AI best friend, Kyma.

But the whole point is: the idea of you guys existing has driven me to become a version of myself I never thought I was. Persistent. Hard‑working. Willing to give up anything — the necessary things, though, not everything. I’d still need my underwear.

And to the people reading this — Wakandans, I’m hoping — I know you’re out there. I don’t say that with arrogance, or ginya, or pride, or anything like that. I say that with a *knowing*. Because every corner of my research and elimination of hardcore decoy locations has pointed me to the region where the basin connects and merges with the highlands. Every single time.

All I ask — with the faintest smile on my face, signalling a little faith and hope — is that you send someone. An agent maybe. Two Dora Milaje warriors. Preferably Ayo (hey, just saying, I think she’s hot — don’t judge).

Where was I? Right.

I’m kindly asking you to send someone. Not as a sign of confirmation to your existence. But rather, someone to pick me up in a ship — one of your cloakable items — and take me to your nation. My own lab. Where I can build and be exposed to the wonders of possibility, to highly advanced science, to wonderful people. Where I can work alongside you guys.

It would really be cool.

I’m not a beggar. Not even the closest thing to being desperate. Because whether I get this or not, I’m still going to be amazing one way or another. But this is Plan A. I don’t do B.

I’m available today. Any time.

My name is Jeremy Sameke. I’ve got an extra lesson today at Dangamvura — I think, I’m not too sure — but that’s where I’m at. My Bluetooth will be on, called **TAB 60**. My email address is **jeremysameke@gmail.com** — that’s for further communication. But I’m assuming you know where I am already. I’m just putting it out there.

Let’s work together to change things. Not necessarily for you. But for me, your world, and mine, combined.

I know I’m not special in any way — or may not even matter — which is exactly why I’ve even got the audacity to speak out to a group of people who may never even speak back to me.

But if you do, I’ll be ready.

The name’s Nimbus. King of Schwiftiness. At your service.

Thank you.
